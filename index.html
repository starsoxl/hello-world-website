Fudgy Brownie Recipe

Bring rich chocolate brownies to life with simple pantry ingredients, a quick method, and a little JavaScript for a baking timer and recipe tips.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
  • 8 oz (225g) dark chocolate, chopped
  • 3/4 cup (150g) granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup (50g) light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup (65g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (25g) unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips or chopped nuts (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line an 8x8-inch pan with parchment paper, leaving edges to lift the brownies out.
  2. Melt butter and chocolate together in a heatproof bowl over simmering water, or microwave in 20-second bursts, stirring until smooth.
  3. Whisk in both sugars, then add eggs one at a time and stir in vanilla.
  4. Fold in flour, cocoa powder, and salt until just combined. Stir in nuts or chips if using.
  5. Pour batter into the prepared pan and smooth the surface. Bake 22–26 minutes until the center is set but still soft.
  6. Cool completely before slicing so the brownies hold their shape.
Quick Tip:
  • For a glossy top, whisk the sugar into the melted chocolate until the mixture is smooth before adding eggs.
  • Use a toothpick to test doneness; a few moist crumbs in the center are perfect.
  • To enhance flavor, add 1 tablespoon of espresso or strong coffee with the vanilla.

Timer: 25:00

Why this recipe works

These brownies are intentionally fudgy. A higher ratio of chocolate and butter to flour keeps the center dense, while just enough eggs create structure and a crackly top.
